LinguaSnapp


LinguaSnapp is a project to develop a multilingual landscape map of Manchester, UK. We are trying to find the hotspots where more than one language is in common use, and what those languages are. This app will help us discover this information, by asking you, when walking down the street, to report any sign, poster, leaflet or other text that is written in more than one language. We are interested in towns and cities from outside Manchester too, in fact anywhere in the world!

Using the LinguaSnapp app is simple. On the main screen simply press the Take Photo button when you see the item of interest, and your device's camera will launch. Once you have taken the photo the app will also record your present coordinates, so we can see exactly where it is located. You will also be asked to input some optional information about the language, location, and of course your translation into English if you know it! Press the Upload button when you are ready, and the photo will be transferred to our servers for examination by the project team. The photo will be deleted from your device once it is uploaded. You can save the photo for later upload too, or delete it. If you submit a photo to LinguaSnapp then you acknowledge our right to publish that image on our website.

Another option on the main screen is Show Pending Uploads, which lists any photos you have not yet uploaded. You can use this screen to go back into the photo record to edit any of the language or location information that you were not able to enter at the time. The Upload All Pending Photos option will send every photo that LinguaSnapp has taken and is still on your device to our servers.

When you upload your photo, it will be sent to our project team for moderation, then published on the LinguaSnapp map. You can see your photo and the photos that others have uploaded on the map here: www.linguasnapp.manchester.ac.uk.

On registering you will be prompted for your email address. This is optional, just used to identify you internally, and allow you to opt-in to our mailing list if you wish. Your details will not be passed on to anybody else.

LinguaSnapp is developed and supported by The University of Manchester.

Please log into the LinguaSnapp server, or register if you do not have or can't remember your password.

This optional screen contains more analytical information for the LinguaSnapp project. See below for the explanation of each drop-down box.

Sector

Public: government or public agency; top-down
Private: individual, commercial; bottom-up

Audience selection

Exclusive: message singles out particular target population
Inclusive: makes message accessible to wider target populations

Language purpose:

Communicative: provides content information
Emblematic: attracts emotional identification

Message function

Landmark: identifies location in relation to history, tradition, customs
Recruitment: engages audience with the agent
Public statement: seeks to influence opinion
Muted: has only accidental value, not intended to relate to specific location (eg displaced label)

Arrangement of languages

Duplicating: repetition of same content
Fragmentary: translation of selected parts
Overlapping: repetition of overall message with different content elements
Complementary: different languages for different aspects of message content
